## ğŸ›¡ï¸ 7. Safety & Privacy at Work

**Rule #1: Your Data is Your Data.**
Follow this workflow before pasting any company data into an AI tool.

<Steps>
### 1. Check the Tool Type ğŸ”
Is it **Public** (ChatGPT Free, Personal Account) or **Enterprise** (Copilot, ChatGPT Enterprise)?
*   **Public:** Training data is collected. **Unsafe** for secrets.
*   **Enterprise:** Data stays in your tenant. Generally safe for work.

### 2. Check Your Policy ğŸ“œ
Even with Enterprise tools, consumer versions often use data for training.
*   **Always check your specific tenant policy.** Some companies opt-out of training even on public tools, while others restrict enterprise tools.

### 3. The Samsung Test ğŸ“±
Ask yourself: *"If this conversation leaked to a competitor tomorrow, would I be fired?"*
If the answer is "Yes," do not type it.
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning">
**The "Samsung Incident":** Engineers pasted proprietary code into a public chatbot to fix it. That code became part of the model's training data. Don't be that guy.
</Callout>

## ğŸ“ 10. The "CO-STAR" Prompt Framework

To get professional results, use a professional structure. A vague prompt gets a vague answer.

| Letter | Meaning | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **C** | **Context** | "I am a Project Manager launching a new app..." |
| **O** | **Objective** | "Write a status update email..." |
| **S** | **Style** | "Concise, bullet points, no fluff..." |
| **T** | **Tone** | "Professional but urgent..." |
| **A** | **Audience** | "For the Executive Team..." |
| **R** | **Response** | "Format as a table with columns for Risk and Mitigation." |

<Callout type="tip">
**Concrete Example:**
Instead of *"Write an email about the delay,"* try:
*"I am a **Project Manager** (Context). Write a **status update** (Objective) for the **Client** (Audience) explaining the server crash. Keep it **apologetic but solution-oriented** (Tone) and use **bullet points** (Style)."*
</Callout>
