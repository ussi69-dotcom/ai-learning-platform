## üõ°Ô∏è 6. Safety & Privacy at Work

**Rule #1: Your Data is Your Data.**
Follow this workflow before pasting any company data into an AI tool.

<Steps>
### 1. Check the Tool Type üîç
Is it **Public** (ChatGPT Free, Personal Account) or **Enterprise** (Copilot, ChatGPT Enterprise)?
*   **Public:** Training data is collected. Unsafe for secrets.
*   **Enterprise:** Data stays in your tenant. Safe for work.

### 2. Assess Data Sensitivity üîê
Are you working with public info (marketing copy) or secret info (roadmap, passwords, PII)?
*   **Never** paste passwords or PII (Personally Identifiable Information) into ANY AI.

### 3. The Samsung Test üì±
Ask yourself: *"If this conversation leaked to a competitor tomorrow, would I be fired?"*
If yes, do not type it.
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning">
**The "Samsung Incident":** Engineers pasted proprietary code into a public chatbot to fix it. That code became part of the model's training data. Don't be that guy.
</Callout>

## üöÄ 7. Efficiency Tips & Tricks

AI is a force multiplier. Here are three hacks to save you an hour today.

### 1. The "Draft, Don't Create" Rule üìù
Never write a document from scratch. Ask AI for an outline or a first draft. It is easier to edit bad text than to write new text from nothing.
> *Prompt: "Draft an agenda for a 30-minute kick-off meeting for Project X."*

### 2. The "Summarize First" Rule üìâ
Before reading a long email chain or a 50-page PDF, ask AI: *"Summarize the conflict in this thread"* or *"What are the 3 key risks in this contract?"*.
*   **Benefit:** You enter the deep reading phase with a mental map of what matters.

### 3. The "Meeting Prep" Hack ü§ù
Before a meeting with a client, ask AI: *"Search the web for recent news about [Company Name]. What are their current challenges?"*
*   **Result:** You walk in looking like you did 2 hours of research.

## üìù 9. The "CO-STAR" Prompt Framework

To get professional results, use a professional structure. A vague prompt gets a vague answer.

| Letter | Meaning | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **C** | **Context** | "I am a Project Manager launching a new app..." |
| **O** | **Objective** | "Write a status update email..." |
| **S** | **Style** | "Concise, bullet points, no fluff..." |
| **T** | **Tone** | "Professional but urgent..." |
| **A** | **Audience** | "For the Executive Team..." |
| **R** | **Response** | "Format as a table with columns for Risk and Mitigation." |

<Callout type="tip">
**Try it:** Next time you need an email, don't just say "Write an email". Say: *"I am a [Context], write a [Objective] for [Audience] in a [Style] style."*
</Callout>
