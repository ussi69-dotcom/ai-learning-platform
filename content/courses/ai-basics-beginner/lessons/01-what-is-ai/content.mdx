## ğŸ”® 5. The "Black Box" Mystery

Here is the scary part. Because the AI learned the patterns by itself (by tuning billions of internal parameters), we don't always know *why* it does what it does.
It is a **Black Box**.

We know the math. We know the code. But we don't know which specific "neuron" fires for the concept of "Love" or "Democracy".

<Diagram type="black-box" />

<Callout type="warning">
**The Probability Trap:** AI does not "know" facts. It predicts the next likely token.
It is **90% accurate**, but **10% hallucination**. Never trust it blindly with critical tasks.
</Callout>

Let's test the limits of the machine. AI models live in the cloud, not in your room.

**Objective:** Prove that the AI doesn't know your *local* context (even if it has web access).

**The Prompt:**

## ğŸ”¬ Lab 3: The Reversal Curse

AI reads text forward, like a stream. It struggles to "look back" or manipulate characters in reverse order.

**Objective:** Trip up the AI with simple string manipulation.

**The Prompt:**
```text
Write the word "Lollipop" backwards.
Then, put a dash between every letter.
```

**Analysis:**
*   **Common Fail:** It might write "p-o-p-i-l-l-o-L" (correct) or mess up the double 'l's.
*   **Harder Version:** "Reverse the sentence 'AI is great' word by word, but keep the letters in each word forward."
*   **Why?** To the AI, "Lollipop" is one token. Splitting it requires "thinking" in characters, which is computationally expensive for it.
