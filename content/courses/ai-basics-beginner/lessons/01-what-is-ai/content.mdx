## üìú 1. The Impossible Dream (A History of "Bombs")

The story of AI isn't a straight line. It's a series of explosions.

<Diagram type="ai-history-timeline" />

### üí£ The First Bomb: Alan Turing (1950)
It started with a question that sounded insane in 1950: **"Can machines think?"**
Alan Turing, the man who cracked the Enigma code and saved millions in WWII, proposed a test. If a machine can talk to you, and you can't tell it's a machine... does it matter if it has a soul?
*The seed was planted.*

### ‚ùÑÔ∏è The Long Winter
But then... silence. For decades, AI was a joke.
Scientists promised robot butlers. We got calculators. Funding dried up. Hope died. This was the **AI Winter**. Everyone thought the dream was impossible.

### üëÅÔ∏è The Awakening: ImageNet (2012)
Then came the moment that changed everything. A team of researchers (including Geoffrey Hinton) built a neural network called **AlexNet**.
They didn't program it to see cats. They showed it millions of images.
And for the first time in history, a computer **LOOKED** at a picture and **KNEW** what it was.
*The eye opened.*

### üöÄ The Explosion: ChatGPT (2022)
The rest is history you lived through. The logic of machines met the creativity of art.

## ü§ñ 2. The Old Republic vs. The New Order

To understand AI, you must unlearn what you know about computers.

### The Old Way: Traditional Software (The Calculator)
For 50 years, computers were like **C-3PO**. Rigid. Polite. Anxious.
If a programmer wrote: `if x > 10, do y`, the computer obeyed. **100% of the time.**
*   üíª **The Logic:** Explicit Rules.
*   üë®‚Äçüíª **The Creator:** Humans write every line.
*   üöß **The Limit:** It can't do what it wasn't told.

### The New Way: Machine Learning (The Student)
AI is like a **Jedi Youngling**.
We don't give it rules. We give it **DATA**.
We show it 10,000 photos of cats. The AI figures out what a "cat" is by itself.

## üîÆ 5. The "Black Box" Mystery

Here is the scary part.
We built the brain. We know the math. But we don't know *exactly* how it thinks.

The AI learned by tuning billions of internal knobs (parameters). Which knob represents "love"? Which one represents "truth"?
We don't know. It is a **Black Box**.

<Callout type="warning">
**The Probability Trap:** AI acts confident, but it is just predicting the next word.
It is **90% accurate**, but **10% hallucination**.
Never trust it blindly. Always verify.
</Callout>

## üî¨ Lab 3: The Socratic Teacher

AI doesn't have to be an encyclopedia. It can be **Socrates**‚Äîsomeone who teaches through questions, not answers. When AI switches to "Socratic questioning" mode, it becomes a thinking tool, not a knowledge tool.

**Objective:** Experience AI as an interactive tutor that guides you to discover answers yourself.

**The Prompt:**
```text
I want to learn how PHOTOSYNTHESIS works. But please, DON'T explain it directly.

Play the role of SOCRATES. You're an ancient Greek philosopher who teaches through QUESTIONS.
You should:

1. Ask me about what I KNOW (or think I know)
2. Lead me with questions toward contradictions (where I realize I'm unsure)
3. Gradually push me toward the answer I should discover myself
4. Be PATIENT and never lecture me directly

Start with a simple question about plants and sunlight.
Wait for my response - don't write my answers for me.
```

**Analysis:**
The AI should respond something like:

*Socrates: "Greetings, curious friend. Tell me - have you ever wondered why plants turn their faces toward the sun each day? What do you think they are seeking?"*

[You respond]

*Socrates: "Interesting! So you say they want warmth. Tell me then - do all living things that seek warmth also need light? Or might warmth and light be different things?"*

[Dialog continues...]

**üí° Aha Moment:** "I don't have to be fed answers. I can force AI to help me THINK. This is the most powerful thing I can do with AI‚Äînot getting information, but developing my thinking process."
